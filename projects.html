Vectorized Entropy-Based Wordle Solver

An information-theoretic solver for NYT Wordle that treats each game as sequential Bayesian inference, choosing the guess that maximizes expected entropy gain over the posterior of remaining candidates. A two-tier candidate model falls back from the 2,315 curated answers to the full 12,972-word legal list, averaging 3.483 guesses per game — beating the human average of 3.92.

Sparse Attack Detection in Power Networks via Lasso Optimization

Sensor attack detection formulated as sparse recovery: L1-regularized regression over measurements corrupted by Gaussian noise and sparse adversarial injections. Across 100 randomized Monte Carlo trials, achieved exact support recovery — every attacked node and line, zero false positives — in 95% of trials.
